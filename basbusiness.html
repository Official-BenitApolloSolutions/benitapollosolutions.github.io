Memorandum of Understanding

Legal Review and Advice on the Memorandum of Understanding (MOU)

Here’s a breakdown of the MOU, along with some key observations and advice:

1. Purpose and Roles
    Purpose Clause: Clearly defines the collaborative goal, which is crucial for alignment.
    Roles and Responsibilities:
  • Programmer: Responsibilities are clearly outlined, focusing on technical tasks.
  • Ideologist: Focuses on app ideation and user experience.
  • Enforcer: Acts as a project manager and mediator.
Advice:
  • Ensure that the role descriptions are comprehensive and include specific deliverables. For example, the Programmer might need to deliver periodic progress reports or bug fixes.
2. Ownership and Profit Sharing
    Profit Distribution:
  • Programmer: 36%
  • Ideologist: 42%
  • Enforcer: 27%
Advice:
  • The profit-sharing allocation should be reviewed to ensure it’s fair. If the Programmer has a long-term maintenance role, this could justify a higher percentage over time.
  • Clarify whether these shares are gross or net profit.
3. Intellectual Property (IP)
  • The MOU states that IP will be shared according to profit shares.
Advice:
  • Specify what happens if a Partner exits—how is their IP ownership addressed?
  • Include a clause regarding licensing or sublicensing of the IP in case of future partnerships or sales.
4. Confidentiality
  • Confidentiality lasts for 4 years post-termination.
Advice:
  • This is a reasonable duration, but clarify exceptions, e.g., disclosures required by law or prior written consent.
5. Decision-Making and Dispute Resolution
  • Mediation and Arbitration: Mediation led by the Enforcer; unresolved disputes go to arbitration under Ghanaian law.
Advice:
  • Good inclusion. Ensure arbitration details are explicit (e.g., arbitration center, language, and arbitrator selection process).
6. Termination
  • Exit Clause: Exiting Partners forfeit profits and pay 3% of the entire project cost.
Advice:
  • Clarify how “project cost” is defined—does it include sunk costs or only future costs?
  • Consider adding flexibility for Partners leaving due to unforeseen circumstances (e.g., health issues).
7. Miscellaneous
  • Amendments: Any changes require written consent from all.
  • Governing Law: Ghanaian law applies.
Advice:
  • Ensure the governing law aligns with where the project operates.
  • You may want to specify a court of jurisdiction in case arbitration doesn’t resolve disputes.
General Recommendations:
  1. Clarify Timelines: Include milestones with deadlines for development phases.
  2. Add a Non-Compete Clause: Prevent Partners from starting competing projects for a set period.
  3. Formalize Deliverables and Reporting: This ensures accountability.
  4. Independent Legal Review: Each Partner should consult independent legal counsel before signing.
